How Victorian Students Travel to School

Locations of schools and the ways students move around Victoria

This visualisation explores how primary and secondary students across Victoria travel to school. Using official Victorian Government datasets, we examine where schools are located, how many exist in each Local Government Area (LGA), and how various modes of travel appear to assist different students in getting to school.

School Locations in Victoria.

This choropleth map shows the number of schools in each Victorian LGA in 2025. It is expected that there would be a higher concentration of schools in the suburbun regions towards the city in comparison to outer regions of Victoria. However, there is a notable amount of schools in rural areas like Mildura or East Gippsland which can indicate a higher migration of families with students towards particular rural areas over the years.

How Students Travel.

The preferred mode of travel for both Primary and Secondary school students appears to be travelling as a vehicle passenger. However, more Primary school students than Secondary school students travel this way while there are a vastly greater number of Secondary school students who take the train indicating a greater independance in travelling for older Victorian students.

Distance and Duration.

In comparing the average travel time and distance for each mode, students who walk to school reach school faster travelling a shorter distance while students who take the train or school travel further and longer. Between the two main school levels, Secondary school students generally tend to travel longer and further to school than Primary school students which can indicate many factoring reasons like age and level of indepenance, including the much greater number of Primary schools in Victoria.
